Inside the Firewall

A firewall is a gatekeeper. It checks network traffic and applies rules to allow, deny, or log connections based on security policy.

Secure Sign In Emergency Lockdown

Key Terms

  • Inbound: traffic entering your network.
  • Outbound: traffic leaving your network.
  • Ports: service “doors” (80/443 web, 22 SSH).
  • Logging: recorded events for audit/response.

Rule types

Specific rules first, broad rules last.

Allow

Permit required traffic (example: HTTPS on port 443).

Deny

Block risky or unnecessary traffic.

Log

Record events for visibility and investigation.

Mini checklist

  • Default deny (least privilege).
  • Only open the ports you need.
  • Log sign-ins and admin actions.
  • Review rules regularly.

Lockdown mode

During an incident, temporarily restrict inbound traffic to essentials only, then restore services safely.
